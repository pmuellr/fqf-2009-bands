2009 French Quarter Festival Favorites

Friday
Jackson Square 11:00-12:40 Connie Jones and The FQF All-Stars - New Orleans native trumpeter Conrad "Connie" Jones III joined the Basin Street Six with Pete Fountain in 1952. In 1967, he became a full-fledged member of Pete Fountain's band, departing seven years later to reorganize and lead the Dukes of Dixieland.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Abita 11:00-12:00 Brother Tyrone and The Mindbenders - Brother Tyrone is a soulful shouter from the West Bank. His band is tighter than your grandmother's girdle as their latest eponymous CD exhibits.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Harrah's 11:00-12:30 Marc Stone Band - Blues guitarist Marc Stone and his sizzling slide have made the Old Point Bar his home away from home, playing and presenting special events there. This is a rare East Bank performance for him.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Capital One 11:15-12:45 Bone Tone - The Bone Tone Brass Band is a solid favorite and old time brass band famous for its martial rhythms and stamina when it parades.
Old US Mint - Crystal Hot Sauce 11:15-13:00 Waylon Thibodeaux and The Bourbon Street Cajuns - Waylon is a highly energetic fiddler who specializes in zydeco and Cajun music.
Old US Mint - Southern Comfort 11:15-12:45 Margie Perez - Margie Perez, the Creole Nightingale of Frenchmen Street, is one of the hardest working musicians in New Orleans.
Preservation Hall 12:00-13:00 Loose Marbles - Street musicians with an interest in trad jazz, or trad jazz musician who love the Great Outdoors? Whatever, they're a great band with a killer singer, Sarah Quintana.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Abita 12:15-13:30 Billy Iuso and The Restless Natives - A rockin' jam band that keep that nasty New Orleans funk alive.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Harrah's 12:45-14:00 Gal Holiday and The Honky Tonk Revue - Gal Holiday and the Honky Tonk Revue formed in the summer of 2004 to revive that classic honky tonk, rockabilly, and country sound with a touch of swing to get you moving.
Dutch Alley 12:45-13:45 Jack Brass - This Minnesota-based brass band is heavily inspired by the New Orleans brass tradition.
Jackson Square 13:00-14:40 Banu Gibson and The New Orleans Hot Jazz - Vocalist/banjoist Banu Gibson leads her own talented band, formed in 1981, on swinging jazz of the 1920s, 1930s and 1940s.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Capital One 13:00-14:30 Original Royal Players - The brass band concept has gone through some serious evolution, but the Royal Players are committed to the tradition.
Old US Mint - Southern Comfort 13:00-14:15 Soul Project - Leader Cristian Duque takes the lessons he learned from playing with Walter "Wolfman" Washington and combines it with Meters-esque funk to make one of the more authentic New Orleans sounding bands around.
French Market 13:15-15:15 Lionel Ferbos and the Louisiana Shakers - Lionel Ferbos, who is nearing his 100th birthday, has played trumpet in New Orleans for over 70 years. "I stick close to the melody as a rule; I'm a melody man," Ferbos says.
Old US Mint - Crystal Hot Sauce 13:20-15:00 Jonno and Bayou Deville - This fiddler blends Americana and blues with a classic Cajun feel for a fresh sound.
Preservation Hall 13:30-14:30 Tornado Brass Band - The Tornado Brass Band emerged from the famed Hurricane Brass Band under Darryl Adams leadership. Members of the Tornado Brass Band went on to start the Dirty Dozen.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Abita 13:45-15:00 Fredy Omar Con Su Banda - New Orleans' leading Latin heartthrob leads an exemplary Latin orchestra.
Dutch Alley 14:00-15:45 Magnolia Brass Band - Started in 1972, this Canadian brass band plays a style known as "Revival Jazz," which blends classic jazz music with rhythm and blues.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Harrah's 14:15-15:30 The Tin Men - The Tin Men is a trio featuring Alex McMurray on guitar, Washboard Chaz Leary on washboard and vocals, and Matt Perrine on sousaphone. The group is quite eclectic, performing everything from jazz standards to modern funk covers.
Old US Mint - Southern Comfort 14:30-15:45 Honey Island Swamp Band - The Honey Island Swamp Band built their roots rock barroom sound by backing up Eric Lindell for several years.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Capital One 14:45-16:15 Audacity Brass Band - Another fine example of the city's brass band tradition.
Jackson Square 15:00-16:40 Pfister Sisters - The Pfister Sisters carry on the traditional the Boswell Sisters created in New Orleans in the '20s and '30s. They have sass and class and a great sense of humor.
Preservation Hall 15:00-16:00 Tornado Brass Band - The Tornado Brass Band emerged from the famed Hurricane Brass Band under Darryl Adams leadership. Members of the Tornado Brass Band went on to start the Dirty Dozen.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Abita 15:15-16:30 Irene Sage Band - Irene Sage has a raging, powerful voice to go with her passionate original songs. Sage is a veteran of French Quarter stages, and her live performances show that.
Old US Mint - Crystal Hot Sauce 15:20-17:00 Feufollet - Another addition to the great trend of young folks throwing down Acadiana waltzes and bayou two-steps with a more modern sound and twist. It's not your grandfather's Cajun, but he can still dance to it.
French Market 15:30-17:30 Lars Edegran Tribute to Danny and Blu Lu Barker - The Danish pianist and long time New Orleans resident plays a tribute to the influential musical couple Danny and Blue Lu Barker. Go and get your leg felt.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Harrah's 15:45-17:15 Higher Heights - Higher Heights combines reggae, dancehall, and rock steady into a heady mix of Jamaican sounds.
Old US Mint - Southern Comfort 16:00-17:30 Posse featuring Kevin O'Day and Reggie Scanlan - Posse features two of the Radiators with powerhouse drummer Kevin O'Day. They play swampy New Orleans funk combined with raspy blues.
Dutch Alley 16:00-17:45 Wolfgang and His New Orleans Band - This Austrian jazz band was inspired by New Orleans traditional jazz.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Capital One 16:30-18:00 The Forgotten Souls Brass Band - The Forgotten Souls Brass Band consists of some of the top players in the brass band scene. Their repertoire of both traditional standards and funky new tunes gets the crowd shaking.
Preservation Hall 16:30-17:30 Doc Houlind Revival Jazz Band - This Danish traditional jazz band play New Orleans jazz across Europe, the Middle East and Asia.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Abita 16:45-18:00 Coco Robicheaux - Legendary New Orleans blues guitarist is native to the Louisiana swamps, and his music pulls together the city's soul and mystery.
Jackson Square 17:00-18:30 The Nobles - Almost 50 years old, Louisiana Hall of Famers the Nobles are one of New Orleans' premier cover bands.
Old US Mint - Crystal Hot Sauce 17:20-19:00 Bruce Daigrepont Cajun Band - A New Orleans-reared Cajun, the self-taught accordion player has hosted the Sunday Cajun session at Tipitina's for decades.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Harrah's 17:30-19:00 The Zydepunks - The Zydepunks play traditional and European folk tunes at whirlwind speed with a punk rock backbeat. Their highly energetic live shows giving them a reputation as one of the best live bands in the city.
Old US Mint - Southern Comfort 17:45-19:00 Big Blue Marble - Big Blue Marble's rock/pop shows the band to be one of the best bands in the strong indie rock movement in New Orleans.
Preservation Hall 18:00-19:00 New Bumpers Revival Jazz Band - France's best trad jazz band makes the pilgrimage here to Preservation Hall. They specialize in the classic Hot 5s and 7s repertoire.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Abita 18:15-19:30 Big Sam's Funky Nation - Trombone player Big Sam Williams merges the brass band sound with electric funk, and he does it with style and volume. His newest disc is Peace, Love and Understanding.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Capital One 18:15-20:00 Soul Rebels - While most brass bands seem to be cut from the same piece of cotton, the Soul Rebels are tailor-made from the finest silk and sometimes come equipped with turntablists, rappers and dancers.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Harrah's 19:30-21:00 Otra - None
Woldenberg Riverfront - Abita 19:45-21:00 Walter "Wolfman" Washington and the Roadmasters - Probably the busiest musician in New Orleans, he's one of the city's premier blues guitarists. Honed from years at dens of iniquity like the Dew Drop Inn, Dorothy's Medallion, and the Maple Leaf Bar, the Wolfman's act is old school soul. He is the real deal.
Saturday
Jackson Square 11:00-12:40 Tim Laughlin - Tim Laughlin is a follower of Pete Fountain, but he adds a contemporary, intelligent edge to his traditional jazz clarinet work.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Abita 11:00-12:00 Spirit of New Orleans Choir - The Spirit of New Orleans Choir is an energetic singing group guaranteed to give you inspiration to dance the day away.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Harrah's 11:00-12:30 Christian Serpas and Ghost Town - A revved up version of country and rockabilly, the band has been described as Led Zeppelin playing Johnny Cash.

Royal Street - 400 11:00-12:30 Bamboula 2000 - Bamboula 2000 fuses rhythmic music from around the planet, from the ancient Mali Empire to the Space Age. Leader Luther Gray was one of the cultural activists who secured Congo Square's place on the National Register of Historic Places.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Capital One 11:15-12:45 Excelsior Brass Band - None
Old US Mint - Crystal Hot Sauce 11:15-13:00 Jeremy and the Zydeco Hotboys - Jeremy began playing music with his cousin, popular Louisiana Zydeco player Geno Delafose, at the age of 13. Shortly afterward, Jeremy formed the Zydeco Hot Boyz and started playing all over Louisiana.
Old US Mint - Southern Comfort 11:15-12:45 The Revealers - The Revealers' raspy vocals and deep skank rhythms keep the sounds of reggae alive in New Orleans. These regulars on the Frenchmen Street scene have been playing since before the Blue Nile was Cafe Istanbul.
Bourbon Street - 700 11:15-13:15 Eddie Bayard and the New Orleans Jazz Orchestra - Bayard is a talented cornet player who has years of experience.
Preservation Hall 12:00-13:00 Panorama Jazz Band - Led by clarinetist Ben Schenck, the Panorama performs jazz, as well as klezmer music, Caribbean biguines and Balkan rhapsodies.
Royal Street - 500 12:00-13:30 The Heritage School of Music - Legendary educator and musician Kidd Jordan directs this high school-aged jazz band that plays better than most college bands.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Abita 12:15-13:30 Van Broussard - Broussard helped create the "swamp pop" sound and is considered a legend in the bayou areas of Louisiana.
Chartres Street 12:30-13:15 Opera a la Carte - Two singer-actors take the audience on a lively journey through the "greatest hits" of opera, including The Barber of Seville, Carmen, Hansel and Gretel, II Trovatore, and The Magic Flute.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Harrah's 12:45-14:00 Marva Wright - Born and raised in New Orleans, big-voiced blues belter Marva Wright is a perpetual crowd-pleaser.
Royal Street - 400 12:45-14:15 John Autin - Pianist and Rabadash Records' head John Autin has had a long running gig at the Hotel Monteleone in their piano bar, but he is far from the schmaltzy cocktail pianist.
Jackson Square 13:00-14:40 Swingaroux - Swingaroux is a local big band that specializes in traditional jazz and swing, occasionally giving the 1930s treatment to songs by Norah Jones and Aretha Franklin.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Capital One 13:00-14:30 Storyville Stompers - The Stompers, founded in 1981, are staples for celebrations of all kinds especially in the French Quarter. Tuba man Woody Penouilh leads the parade.
Old US Mint - Southern Comfort 13:00-14:15 Kayla Woodson and Louisiana Lightning - This country vocalist is only age 14, but she is already playing gigs with her backing band Louisiana Lightning.
Dutch Alley 13:00-14:45 Canal Creepers - None
Bourbon Street - 300 13:00-16:00 The University of New Orleans Jazz Orchestra - The University of New Orleans Jazz Studies department is one of the best in the country. Graduates range from Nicholas Payton to Jessie McBride, to Joshua Paxton, so if you want to see the jazz hitmakers of tomorrow, this is the place to see them before they break out.
Bourbon Street - 311 13:00-15:00 Evan Christopher's Clarinet Road - Virtuoso clarinet player Evan Christopher frequently plays with Tom McDermott and/or the Danza Quartet. For this set, he leads the band.
French Market 13:15-15:15 The Last Straws - This traditional New Orleans jazz band has been playing together for nearly half a century.
Bourbon Street - 600 13:15-15:15 The New Orleans Moonshiners - The New Orleans Moonshiners play both New Orleans traditional standards and some "hot" French jazz.
Old US Mint - Crystal Hot Sauce 13:20-15:00 Lost Bayou Ramblers - Broussard, Louisiana's native Lost Bayou Ramblers play Cajun music with a youthful energy and enthusiasm.
Preservation Hall 13:30-14:30 Doc Houlind Revival Jazz Band - This Danish traditional jazz band play New Orleans jazz across Europe, the Middle East and Asia.
Bourbon Street - 700 13:30-15:30 Clive Wilson's New Orleans Serenaders - Originally from London, Wilson moved to New Orleans in the early '70s and studied music at Loyola during the day and played in a jazz band on Bourbon Street at night. Now he is one of the city's most well known trumpet players.
Chartres Street 13:30-14:45 Old Wine in New Bottles - Led by Loyola University clarinet instructor John Reeks, Old Wine in New Bottles re-invented the works of Haydn, Mozart, and Brahms for the clarinet.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Abita 13:45-15:00 Casa Samba Extravaganza - This dance and rhythm group is New Orleans' most popular Brazilian entertainment.

Sunday
Jackson Square 11:00-12:40 Dukes of Dixieland - Founded in 1948 by the Assunto brothers (Frank, who died in 1974, and Fred, who died in 1966), the Dukes hold the distinction of being the first jazz band to record a stereophonic album. Pete Fountain was an early member of the band and the Dukes recorded with Louis Armstrong during the late '50s. The present group is related to the Assuntos' band by name only.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Abita 11:00-12:00 Thelonius Monk Institute of Jazz - In 2007, the Thelonious Monk Institute of Jazz moved to the campus of Loyola University New Orleans where the program has been flourishing ever since. Its graduate students now perform regularly as a jazz ensemble.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Harrah's 11:00-12:30 The Friendly Travelers - The gospel sounds of the Friendly Travelers used to grace the ears of coffee drinkers in the late, lamented Kaldi's coffeehouse. Their shimmering harmonies of praise get better with age.
Woldenberg Riverfront - Capital One 11:00-12:45 Society Brass Band - A brass band show with the little people in mind.
French Market 11:00-13:00 "Kid" Simmons' New Orleans Jazz Band - Simmons keeps New Orleans music alive with his traditional brass band.
Dutch Alley 11:00-12:45 Doc Houlind - This Danish traditional jazz band play New Orleans jazz across Europe, the Middle East and Asia.
